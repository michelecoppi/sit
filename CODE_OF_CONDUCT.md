# Consortium Code of Conduct

Issued by the International SIT Consortium. Applies to every repository,
issue, pull request, RFC discussion and conference hallway conversation
(SITCON included) under the SIT Standard project.

## 1. Purpose

The Consortium exists to promote the evolution of symbolic information
technology. That mission fails if contributors treat each other badly.
This document exists so it doesn't come to that.

## 2. Expected Behavior

- Be respectful of differing viewpoints, even about whether `6` should come
  before `7`.
- Give and accept constructive feedback gracefully.
- Focus on what is best for the community and the Standard, not the person.
- Show empathy toward other contributors, especially the Department of
  Legacy Technologies — someone still has to maintain ASCII compatibility.

## 3. Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks, public or private.
- Trolling, deliberate derailment, or bad-faith arguments.
- Publishing others' private information without consent.
- Any conduct that would be inappropriate in a professional setting.

Satire is welcome. It is the entire point of this project. Satire aimed at
another contributor instead of at the fictional Consortium is not.

## 4. Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards of acceptable behavior
and will take appropriate action in response to any instance of unacceptable
behavior, including warning, removal of comments/PRs, or a temporary or
permanent ban from the project.

## 5. Reporting

Report unacceptable behavior by opening a private security advisory or
contacting the maintainers directly (see [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) for
contact channel). Reports are handled confidentially, and faster than the
Working Group's usual 67-day meeting cycle.
