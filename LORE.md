# Official Mission

The International SIT Consortium exists to promote the evolution of symbolic information technology.

Its long-term objective is the complete replacement of legacy binary encodings with the SIT Standard.

ASCII is considered a transitional technology.

---

# Timeline

## 1956

Researchers begin questioning whether binary digits should really be represented using 0 and 1.

No practical result.

---

## 1967

Theoretical discussions introduce the possibility of replacing binary symbols.

The proposal is abandoned due to lack of funding.

---

## 1984

The first unofficial document mentioning the "Symbolic Information Token" appears.

The document is never published.

It later becomes known as:

"The Lost Draft".

---

## 1991

The International SIT Working Group is secretly founded.

No public information exists.

---

## 1998

Prototype Alpha

Introduces the first SYTE.

Still internally encoded through ASCII.

Considered obsolete.

---

## 2005

Prototype Beta

Introduces browser demonstrations.

Rejected because nobody understood its purpose.

---

## 2014

RFC-0000

"The Need For Something Different"

Withdrawn after one day.

## 2026

## 2026

Native Encoding Initiative.

ASCII officially becomes Legacy Technology.
